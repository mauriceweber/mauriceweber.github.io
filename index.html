About me

I am a second year PhD student at ETH Zurich’s DS3Lab, supervised by Professor Ce Zhang.

My research interests revolve around the reliability of machine learning and quantum algorithms. In the context of machine learning, I am interested in robustness guarantees against data perturbations which entails the adversarial case, but also other scenarios where perturbations may arise from sources such as semantic transformations or naturally occurring noise in quantum systems. In a similar vein, in the area of quantum algorithms, I work on establishing principled approaches to reason about their reliability under in noisy scenarios.

I hold a BSc and an MSc in Mathematics, both from ETH Zürich, where I focused on statistics and machine learning and graduated in 2019 with distinction.

Feel free to contact me by email or on Twitter and LinkedIn.
